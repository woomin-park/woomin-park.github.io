My current research explores the normative dimensions of inquiry and the foundations of _epistemic psychology_. The prevailing view often dismisses the _epistemic normativity_ of inquiry on the grounds that inquiry is an action rather than a belief. Against this, I believe that the domain of epistemic normativity validly extends beyond beliefs, and the normativity of inquiry is a _hybrid_ normativity that combines instrumental normativity and epistemic normativity. Furthermore, I propose that just as moral psychology is foundational to ethics, epistemic psychology is vital for epistemology. Because this field remains largely unexplored, my current work aims to lay its groundwork by investigating the distinction between epistemic and conventional norms. By comparing to the well-known moral/conventional distinction, I believe that a robust epistemic/conventional distinction is not only meaningful but essential for undertaking epistemic psychology. Even so, I remain deeply drawn to the metaphysical implications of physics and the relationship between physics and the other special sciences.

Before pursuing philosophy, Woomin trained to be a physics teacher at [Korea National University of Education](https://www.knue.ac.kr/phys/). He then earned a B.A. in philosophy from [Yonsei University](https://philosophy.yonsei.ac.kr) and an M.A. in philosophy from [Seoul National University](https://philosophy.snu.ac.kr).

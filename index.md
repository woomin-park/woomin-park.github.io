Park, Woomin (pronounced [pag-u-min] in Korean) is [a Ph.D. student in the Department of Philosophy at Purdue University](https://www.cla.purdue.edu/directory/profiles/woomin-park.html). 

My research sits at the intersection of metaphysics, epistemology, and philosophy of science.  Recently, my research has focused on the normative dimensions of inquiry and the foundations of _epistemic psychology_. Against the prevailing view that the epistemic normativity of inquiry is a non-starter—often argued because inquiry is an action rather than a belief—I believe that there are  indeed epistemic norms that govern inquiries. The normativity of inquiry is a _hybrid_ normativity that combines instrumental normativity and epistemic normativity. Furthermore, just as moral psychlogy is essential to ethics, epistemic psychology holds similar importance for epistemology. Yet, to my knowledge, this project has not been systematically undertaken. To build this foundation, I am investigating the epistemic/conventional distinction. I suggest that this distinction is conceptually analogous to the moral/conventional distinction regarding normativity. Ultimately, I want to argue that drawing a meaningful boundary between the epistemic and the conventional is both possible and highly useful for undertaking epistemic psychology. Even so, I am still drawn to the metaphysical implications of physics, and the relation of physics to the other special sciences. My earlier work explored the possibility of unifying metaphysical grounding and causation, focusing on the challenges posed by grounding necessitarianism.

Before pursuing philosophy, Woomin trained to be a physics teacher at [Korea National University of Education](https://www.knue.ac.kr/phys/). He then earned a B.A. in philosophy from [Yonsei University](https://philosophy.yonsei.ac.kr) and an M.A. in philosophy from [Seoul National University](https://philosophy.snu.ac.kr). Following his master's degree, he taught high school physics as a part-time instructor and served as a teaching associate of humanities at [Pohang University of Science and Technology (POSTECH)](https://hss.postech.ac.kr). Before coming to Purdue, he was a doctoral student in philosophy at [Seoul National University](https://philosophy.snu.ac.kr).

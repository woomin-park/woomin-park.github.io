Park, Woomin (pronounced [pag-u-min] in Korean) is [a Ph.D. student in the Department of Philosophy at Purdue University](https://www.cla.purdue.edu/directory/profiles/woomin-park.html). 

</div>

</div>

My research sits at the intersection of metaphysics, epistemology, and philosophy of science. My earlier work explored the possibility of unifying metaphysical grounding and causation, focusing on the challenges posed by grounding necessitarianism. Recently, I am  interested in the normative dimensions of inquiry and the foundations of _epistemic psychology_. Against the prevailing view that the epistemic normativity of inquiry is a non-starter, I believe that there are epistemic norms of inquiry. Therefore, there is a place for epistemic normativity. Furthermore, by drawing an analogy to moral psychology, I explore the distinction between epistemic and conventional norms. I want to argue that treating epistemic norms as a type of social norm provides a meaningful and fruitful framework for undertaking epistemic psychology—a prospective field I aim to develop further. Even so, I am still drawn to the metaphysical implications of physics, and the relation of physics to the other special sciences.

Before pursuing philosophy, Woomin trained to be a physics teacher at [Korea National University of Education](https://www.knue.ac.kr/phys/). He then earned a B.A. in philosophy from [Yonsei University](https://philosophy.yonsei.ac.kr) and an M.A. in philosophy from [Seoul National University](https://philosophy.snu.ac.kr). Following his master's degree, he taught high school physics as a part-time instructor and served as a teaching associate of humanities at [Pohang University of Science and Technology (POSTECH)](https://hss.postech.ac.kr). Before coming to Purdue, he was a doctoral student in philosophy at [Seoul National University](https://philosophy.snu.ac.kr).

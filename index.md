Park, Woomin (pronounced [pag-u-min] in Korean) is [a Ph.D. student in the Department of Philosophy at Purdue University](https://www.cla.purdue.edu/directory/profiles/woomin-park.html). 

My research sits at the intersection of metaphysics, epistemology, and philosophy of science.  Recently, my current research explores the normative dimensions of inquiry and the foundations of _epistemic psychology_. The prevailing view often dismisses the _epistemic normativity_ of inquiry on the grounds that inquiry is an action rather than a belief. Against this, I believe that the normativity of inquiry is a _hybrid_ normativity that combines instrumental normativity and epistemic normativity. There are indeed epistemic norms that govern inquiries. Furthermore, I propose that just as moral psychology is foundational to ethics, epistemic psychology is vital for epistemology. Because this field remains largely unexplored, my current work aims to lay its groundwork by investigating the distinction between epistemic and conventional norms. By comparing to the well-known moral/conventional distinction, I believe that a robust epistemic/conventional distinction is not only meaningful but essential for undertaking epistemic psychology. 

</div>

Even so, I am still drawn to the metaphysical implications of physics, and the relation of physics to the other special sciences. My earlier work explored the possibility of unifying metaphysical grounding and causation, focusing on the challenges posed by grounding necessitarianism.

</div>

Before pursuing philosophy, Woomin trained to be a physics teacher at [Korea National University of Education](https://www.knue.ac.kr/phys/). He then earned a B.A. in philosophy from [Yonsei University](https://philosophy.yonsei.ac.kr) and an M.A. in philosophy from [Seoul National University](https://philosophy.snu.ac.kr). Following his master's degree, he taught high school physics as a part-time instructor and served as a teaching associate of humanities at [Pohang University of Science and Technology (POSTECH)](https://hss.postech.ac.kr). Before coming to Purdue, he was a doctoral student in philosophy at [Seoul National University](https://philosophy.snu.ac.kr).
